Coral Sense is not only about planting corals. It's about restoring our relationship with the ocean — through curiosity, real observation, and the kind of care that only comes from being close to something alive.

Our Coral Gardens

2 gardens.
2 different approaches.

Both programs visit both restoration sites. Each shows a different face of the ocean and a different logic of restoration. Together, they give you the full picture of how a reef lives — and what we can do for it.

Garden 01 · Amed

Coral Sense Research Garden

A high-stress zone: sedimentation, temperature swings, seasonal turbidity. Not a postcard reef — a living observation field for what truly survives under pressure. 10 minutes from Amed — turtles, wreck dives, freediving, Volcano Agung views.

  • Science garden — underwater laboratory
  • Growing highly stress-resistant corals
  • 6 simultaneous scientific experiments
  • Located in the Coral Triangle
  • AI technologies for reef monitoring
Garden 02 · Padang Bai

Padang Bai — Livingseas Partner Site

The largest human-planted coral garden on Bali, built with our partners Livingseas. 500,000+ corals planted, 8,000+ m² of seafloor restored. This is where restoration at scale becomes visible.

Program 2 · Research

A month that feeds your education, your CV, and how you think as a future scientist.

If you're studying marine biology, conservation, or environmental science — this is the deeper track. You dive more often, observe the reef as a system, and start to notice what used to slip past you.

Minimum 4 weeks·$1,580 / month·Marine bio students & early-career
What makes this different: Live experiments — substrate effects, depth variables, species competition. You learn to read a reef as a system, and how AI-assisted tools support real field research.

Who it's for

  • Students of marine biology, marine conservation, environmental science, ecology
  • Early-career participants stepping into reef restoration and marine research
  • Divers ready to grow from recreational diving into disciplined field observation

What you'll actually learn

  • Reef care, structure maintenance, and restoration decision logic
  • Photo protocols, anomaly spotting, basic monitoring methods
  • Coral group identification and reef dynamics observation
  • How AI-assisted tools support field material analysis
  • The difference between recreational diving and science-oriented observation

How four weeks unfold.

01

Foundations

Meet the project, the team, the reef, the structures. Join coral planting and reef care. Start to understand why each restoration decision is made the way it is.

02

Care & Monitoring

Structure maintenance, threats to fragments, anomaly documentation, photo protocols, early indicators of growth, survival, and recovery.

03

Data & AI

Organize observations systematically — coral groups, reef dynamics, fish. Learn how AI tools help process field materials and surface anomalies.

04

Your Own Voice

More independent underwater. Real discussion with the team. When ready — short educational briefings during Coral Restoration Days.
